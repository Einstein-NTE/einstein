Tips & Tricks

 
In this section some tips & tricks are collected for the work with the current version V1.0.06.

Features that are not yet - or not yet fully - working are highlighted and some possible workarounds are mentioned.

1. Data Entry - general issues

a) It is not possible to change the name of an existing equipment, process etc once it has been created and saved. The tool will automatically add a new item to the list with the new name (in addition to the existing ones).
Workaround: If You want to change the name, create a copy with the new name and delete the old one. Take care: Connections from / to this equipment / process / pipe etc. then have to be established again.

b) Fuel data bases

- LCV of natural is in kWh/kg (not in kWh/m3)

c) Refresh of windows

- Lists for selection of parameters are not updated automatically in the questionnaire panels. E.g., if a new fluid is added into the DB while data entering, in order to view the new fluid in the pop up list close and enter again the window in use (e.g. the process questionnaire).

- In order to view a New project or an imported one among the “ project in the database” go to the EINSTEIN main screen and then go back again to “Einstein industry data”

d)  Buildings. Not yet active. But there is a workaround: heating and air conditioning of production halls can be modelled like a process (into the process data sheet) and therefore data on heating and air conditioning of production halls must be collected even though the building calculation module does not exist 

2. Modeling of processes

a) Processes with several incoming and / or outgoing flows (multi-flow processes)

In the current version, it is only possible to model processes with one incoming and one outgoing flow.

Work-around for multi-flow processes:

Take the case that You have processes with two or more incoming different streams (e.g. water and meat) and/or two or more three different outgoing streams (e.g. water, meat, steam).  

Let's treat them separately:
 
A. The INCOMING STREAMS  
In the example of a meat cooker we have two incoming streams:

 The CIRCULATION heat for bringing the incoming streams to process temperature is mdot*cp*(T_process - T_inlet)

- If we want to consider both the meat and the water, we have to define some MIX as "working medium" (-> add as new fluid) whose properties are a weighted average (weighted by thermal mass m*cp)

- If one thermal mass is very small compared to the other, then we just select ONE and depreciate the other.

 The MEDIUM of the incoming streams is the "process medium", and which one depends on what you chose above.

 

B. The OUTGOING ONES

The problem is the same as with the incoming, EINSTEIN allows only for one.

But: while the Incoming streams ALL contribute to the heat demand, the Outgoing streams are only of interest for us, if either their waste heat content is already used, or we think that it MIGHT be used.

The other ones can be just neglected.

So in the specific case of the meat cooker:

- recovering some heat from the outgoing cooked meat should be very difficult, so it will be neglected

- the remaining problem is whether to consider waste heat flow associated to water or to steam  -> Choose the one which is more relevant (energy content, possibility of recovery…).

- if you want to consider both this is - in the present version - rather complicated.

You can define some "mix" fluid X with the c_p value and the condensation temperature set so that

mdot_X*[h_X(out) - h_X(final)] = mdot_steam*[h_vapour(out) - h_condensed_ steam(final)] + mdot_waste_water*cp_waste_water*[(T_waste_water(out) - T_waste_water(final)]

The simplest way to do this and that more or less maintains not only the quantity but also the temperature distribution of the waste heat, would be to adjust the cp of the outgoing vapour (cp_X = cp_water + Delta, T_cond_X = T_cond_vapour), adjusting the unknown Delta until the upper  condition is fulfilled.

Idem, the outgoing medium depends on your choice, can be the waste water, the steam, or the X.

This explanation is limited now to the circulation part of the heat demand: inflows + outflows. Start-up and heat losses (maintenance) may be also take into account.

3. Consistency checking and energy statistics

a) Only annual data are available in V1.0.06. Monthly and daily statistics not yet implemented

b) In order to get familiar with the data input and the consistency-check concept,  it is recommended to try to perform the example 4.2 (Project Einstein Guide 4.2 Basecase) according the description given within the Audit Guide at pages 92-97.

Following a simple procedure for data entering may help to pass the consistency check in a shorter time.

It is recommended to enter the process’ data first, then the equipment’s (but without any boundary such as the load factor), then the pipes’ and last the overall consumption. Of course in order to perform the consistency check equipments and pipes have to be previously created and linked each other and to the processes – but without adding any specific parameter such as the power etc.

It is also recommended to analyse sub-system by sub-system (e.g. Enter data of equipment 1 -> related pipes -> related processes THEN check the consistency and enter the second group of data  equipment 2 -> related pipes -> related processes…).

4. Benchmark Module

Not yet fully active in V1.0.06. Operations with the benchmark module may cause instabilities. => on your own risk.

5.Heat recovery module

1) The heat exchangers can be seen with all their details, back in the questionnaire sheet "Heat reovery". Here heat exchanger data can be changed and heat exchangers can be deleted.  In case you want to change the size of a heat exchanger, please do so by changing the "total heat transferred" and either adjust the heat transfer rate as well, or leave it empty.
If too many heat exchangers that are connected to the SAME heat sink or heat source are CHANGED IN SIZE, this might cause problems for the calculation, as complexity for the algorithm for splitting streams and keeping the thermodynamic balance correct, increase. In this case the remaining energy demand in the YED curves, might NOT coincide with the remaining energy demand shown in "Heat demand (temp)" in the energy statistics.1)
 
2) Processes that have a "maintainance heat demand" in EINSTEIN, are modelled in the Heat recovery module as LATENT streams. In some cases energy transferred over a heat exchanger that
- has a latent stream as heat sink AND
- when only a part of this latent stream is covered by the heat exchanger
are not substracted from the energy demand curves.

6. Heat and cold supply

1)    COOLING. Cooling is not yet supported in V1.0.06

Possible workaround: Define a process with an equivalent heat demand and an electrical boiler. This allows you (a) to include the electricity demand for chillers in the energy statistics and (b) to evaluate the possibility to cover part of the cooling demand with thermal chillers (absorption, adsorption, etc.) using heat as driving energy.

For the latter purpose, the equivalent heat demand should be defined with
a) equivalent process temperature = average driving temperature for the thermal chiller
b) equivalent heat demand = cooling demand / average COP
c) equivalent efficiency electrical boiler = (COP_electrically driven compression chiller / average COP_thermal chiller)  - (parasitic electricity thermal chiller / heat input thermal chiller)

2)      BIOMASS. Not yet supported in V1.0.06

3) CHP
Only manual design of CHP plants is active. Automatic design not yet supported in V1.0.06

4) BOILERS AND BURNERS

For automatic design of boilers and burners, only natural gas fired boilers are considered.
