9.1: Overview on the TCA

For the company itself the economic analysis of the proposed energy supply is one of the most curcial questions. Therefore emphasis has to be put on this step and the more detailed data can be acquired for this analysis, the higher will be the reliability of the results.

For the economic analysis of a new energy supply systems, operating (energy) costs have to be compared with the current equipment. Therefore, a suitable methodology is to calculate all costs that will occur in the future with the existing heat and cold supply anc compare those with the expected investment and other costs of the proposed alternative energy supply system. The difference in costs gives the expected cash flow that occurs due to the change of the supply equipment.

In general the following cost categories need to be considered:

  • Investment costs

    • offers from suppliers or use of 2nd hand equipment

  • Subsidies and fundings

  • Revenues can be achieved through the sale of the replaced equipment

  • Operating costs

    • Energy costs inkl. expected increase in energy prices

    • Maintainance, Labour costs, Insurance, utilities etc.

  • Contingencies

    • In case the current supply system is not changed: tax disadvantages, costs for remediation acitivities, costs for compliance with legislation, negative impacts on market share, affection by CO2 emission trading etc.

    • In case the energy supply system is changed: tax benefits, positive impacts on markets share, amelioration of company image

  • Non re-occurring costs

    • Repair works for equipments, exchange of collectors, irregular maintainance, permits, legal costs, prevention costs etc.

In conventional cost assessments the focus will lie on the investment costs and operating costs. However, for a consideration of the overall true costs also contigenices and non-reoccuring costs have to be considered and they can have an important impact on ther final result. The cost analysis should be suitable for detection of all parameters that influence the economic performance of energy efficiency and the installation of an energy supply systems in industrial processes, besides the energy costs.

For a total costs assessment  (TCA) that looks at a longer time period and takes into account macro-economic parameters as well, contigencies and non-reoccurring cost are the categories for their consideration.

It is obvious that the following parameters are crucial for the result of the cost assessment:

  • Loan interest rate

  • Company Specific Discount rate

  • Expected development of energy prices

  • Chosen time frame for the economic analysis



The result of the economic analysis includes the investment and the payback period, however should as well include economic parameters that show the economic performance in a longer time period. Here, the internal rate of return and the development of the net present value over the years are highly important.

9.2: Main menu options

At the start page of the TCA module, the most important parameters that have been stated in the questionnaire are shown. For changing these values, please go back to the questionnaire and re-edit your entry.

Further the start page is structured to edit the cost data of

  1. the current heat and cold supply (energy and operating costs, contingencies and non re-occuring costs) and

  2. the proposed heat and cold supply systems of the elaborated alternatives („Proposals“).

For each of the proposals the investment costs, the energy and operating costs, contingencies and the non-reoccuring costs can be edited by clicking on the respective buttons. For details which data can be edited, please have a look at the following chapters 9.3. and 9.4., as there are differences of possible data entry in the different calculation modes of EINSTEIN.

9.3: TCA in automatic calculation mode

The fast calculation is carried out nearly automatically with the information previously provided by the users in the questionnaires and the costs from the pre-design of alternative supply system. The users are only required to give the figure of the possible revenue from the existing equipments to be replaced by the project.

In the fast calculation, it is also impossible for users to change any other figures which are automatically appointed in the calculation for instances the investments of the new processes, the operating and maintenance costs, the project lifetime etc.

As this is a quick and automatic calculation, only main cost categories are taken into account including the investments and subsidies, energy costs, operating and maintenance costs. The contingencies and non-reoccuring costs requiring more detailed information are not included in the calculation.






9.4: TCA in semi-automatic calculation mode

A more manual calculation of TCA is available for the user’s choice. In the semi-automatic and manual mode of EINSTEIN, users can optionally give their own values for the calculation of the costs. The input parameters allow to be changed include the investment and the operating and maintenance costs and the other cost categories to be additionally filled in are the contingencies and non-reoccuring costs.
